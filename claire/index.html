Timeline:
The search for Claire Bell
Missing Sunday. Found Wednesday.
Scroll right →
Sunday, June 15
Claire Bell, 3, is last seen at 9:45 a.m. near Newman Blvd. in LaSalle, Montreal, wearing a white shirt with a red collar, grey pants, and no shoes. Her mother reports her missing six hours later at a fireworks shop in Coteau-du-Lac, about 60 km away. Police mark the apartment door as a protected scene.
Monday, June 16
Police search LaSalle, Coteau-du-Lac, and areas near the Ontario border. A dead dog, believed to be the Chihuahua seen with Claire, is found near Highway 30. A command post is established. Residents are asked to check yards and sheds. No Amber Alert is issued.
Tuesday, June 17
Claire’s mother, Rachel Todd, is arrested early morning and appears in court in the afternoon, charged with child abandonment. SQ continues searching around Vaudreuil-Dorion. Volunteers are not requested to join the search.
Wednesday, June 18
Police report a sighting of Claire near Casselman, Ontario, around 2 p.m. The search continues with 150 SQ officers. A bilingual witness wearing an "Abondance" apron is sought. Claire is found safe by Ontario Provincial Police around 3 p.m.
Wednesday, June 18 – 3 p.m.
Claire Bell is found alive by Ontario Provincial Police near Casselman, 150 km from Montreal. Her father posts: “Thank you everyone. Please allow me and my family this time with our girl.”
