Timeline:
The search for Claire Bell
Missing Sunday. Found Wednesday.
Scroll right →
Sunday, June 15
Claire Bell, 3, is last seen at 9:45 a.m. near Newman Blvd. in LaSalle, Montreal. She is wearing a white shirt with a red collar, grey pants, and no shoes. Her mother reports her missing six hours later at a fireworks shop in Coteau-du-Lac, about 60 km away. Police begin searching her home and mark the apartment door as a protected scene.
Monday, June 16
Morning:
SQ launches a large search near Coteau-du-Lac using helicopters, ATVs, and canine teams. Officers search a 10 km stretch along Highway 20. Residents are asked to check their yards and sheds.
Afternoon:
A dead dog — believed to be Claire’s Chihuahua — is found near Highway 30. The search continues in LaSalle, Coteau-du-Lac, and near the Ontario border. No Amber Alert is issued.
Tuesday, June 17
Morning:
Rachel Todd is arrested and charged with child abandonment in the early hours. SQ officers search near Highway 30. Garbage collection is paused. Volunteers are not being called.
Afternoon:
Todd appears in court by video wearing a red shirt and grey blanket. She says little as the Crown opposes her release. A bail hearing is set for Wednesday. The investigation continues.
Wednesday, June 18
Morning:
Officers search an abandoned sand pit near St-Télesphore and along Highway 30. Police are seen in Pointe-Claire and Coteau-du-Lac. A rescue boat is deployed. Public urged not to join the search.
Afternoon:
SQ confirms a sighting of Claire around 2 p.m. in eastern Ontario. Police appeal for dashcam footage and issue a witness alert for a woman wearing an apron with “Abondance” on it. Todd returns to court.
Wednesday, June 18 – 3 p.m.
Claire Bell is found alive by Ontario Provincial Police near Casselman, Ont. She is safe. The OPP confirms she was found over 150 km from where she was last seen in Montreal.
Her father posts:
“Thank you everyone. Please allow me and my family this time with our girl.”
