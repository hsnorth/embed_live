Timeline:

The search for Claire Bell

Scroll right →

Missing Sunday. Found Wednesday.

Sunday, June 15

Claire Bell, 3, is last seen at 9:45 a.m. near Newman Blvd. in LaSalle, Montreal.

Her mother reports her missing six hours later at a fireworks shop in Coteau-du-Lac, 60 km away.

Police mark the apartment door as a protected scene and begin searching her home.

Monday, June 16

Police search LaSalle, Coteau‑du‑Lac, and areas near the Ontario border.

A dead dog, believed to be Claire’s Chihuahua, is found near Highway 30.

Command posts are set up; residents are asked to check yards and sheds.

Tuesday, June 17

Claire’s mother, Rachel Todd, is arrested early morning and charged with child abandonment.

She appears in court in the afternoon via video; the Crown opposes her release.

Searches continue around Vaudreuil‑Dorion; volunteers are not requested.

Wednesday, June 18

An SQ sighting places Claire near Casselman, Ontario, around 2 p.m.

Searches continue with 150 officers; witnesses include a bilingual woman in an “Abondance” apron.

Later, OPP find her safe near Casselman around 3 p.m.

Wednesday, June 18 – 3 p.m.

Claire Bell is found alive by Ontario Provincial Police, 150 km from Montreal.

Her father posts: “Thank you everyone. Please allow me and my family this time with our girl.”
