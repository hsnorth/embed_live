Timeline:

The search for Claire Bell

Scroll right →

Missing Sunday. Found Wednesday.

Sunday, June 15

  • Last seen at 9:45 a.m. near Newman Blvd., LaSalle, Montreal.
  • Reported missing six hours later at a fireworks shop in Coteau‑du‑Lac (60 km away).
  • Police mark her home as a protected scene and launch initial searches.

Monday, June 16

  • Search expands to LaSalle, Coteau‑du‑Lac, and Ontario border.
  • Dead Chihuahua, believed to be Claire’s, found near Highway 30.
  • Command posts established; residents asked to check yards and sheds.

Tuesday, June 17

  • Morning: Rachel Todd arrested and charged with child abandonment.
  • Afternoon: Todd appears via video; Crown opposes her release.
  • Searches continue around Vaudreuil‑Dorion; volunteers not requested.

Wednesday, June 18

  • Reported sighting near Casselman, Ont., at about 2 p.m.
  • Search by 150 SQ officers with civilian teams.
  • Witness sought: bilingual woman in “Abondance” apron.

Wednesday, June 18 – 3 p.m.

  • Claire Bell found alive by OPP, 150 km from Montreal.
  • Father: “Thank you everyone. Please allow me and my family this time with our girl.”
