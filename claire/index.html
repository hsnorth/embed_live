Timeline:
The search for Claire Bell
Missing Sunday. Found Wednesday.
Scroll right →
Sunday, June 15
Claire Bell, 3, is last seen at 9:45 a.m. near Newman Blvd. in LaSalle, Montreal. She is wearing a white shirt with a red collar, grey pants, and no shoes. Her mother reports her missing six hours later at a fireworks shop in Coteau-du-Lac, about 60 km away. Police begin searching her home and mark the apartment door as a protected scene.
Monday, June 16
Police search LaSalle, Coteau-du-Lac, and areas near the Ontario border. A dead dog, believed to be the Chihuahua seen with Claire, is found near Highway 30. A command post is established. Police urge residents to check their yards. No Amber Alert is issued.
Tuesday Morning, June 17
Claire’s mother, Rachel Todd, is arrested overnight and charged with child abandonment. Police search areas around Vaudreuil-Dorion. A black minibus with officers is seen at the command post. Residents gather outside the fireworks store.
Tuesday Afternoon, June 17
Rachel Todd appears in court via video. She nods throughout and says only “OK” and “bye.” The Crown says more charges could come. Volunteers are not requested to assist with the search.
Wednesday Morning, June 18
Police say Claire was seen in Ontario near Casselman around 2 p.m. Sunday. Search continues with 150 SQ officers and trained civilian teams. Police seek a key witness: a bilingual woman wearing an apron that says "Abondance."
Wednesday Afternoon, June 18
Claire is found safe by Ontario Provincial Police around 3 p.m. Her father posts: “Thank you everyone. Please allow me and my family to [have] this time with our girl.”
