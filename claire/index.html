Timeline:

The search for Claire Bell

Scroll right →

Sunday, June 15

Claire Bell, aged three, was last seen at 9:45 a.m. near Newman Boulevard in LaSalle, Montreal.

Her mother reported her missing six hours later at a fireworks shop in Coteau-du-Lac.

Monday, June 16

  • Search extended to LaSalle, Coteau-du-Lac and the Ontario border.
  • Dead Chihuahua, believed to be Claire’s, found near Highway 30.

Tuesday, June 17

  • Morning: Rachel Todd arrested and charged with child abandonment.
  • Afternoon: Todd appears in court via video; release opposed.

Wednesday, June 18

Claire was spotted near Casselman, Ontario, around 2 p.m.

Ontario Provincial Police later confirmed she was found alive the same afternoon.

Wednesday, June 18 – 3 p.m.

  • Claire Bell was found alive by Ontario Provincial Police, about 150 km from Montreal.
  • Her father said: “Thank you everyone. Please allow me and my family this time with our girl.”
