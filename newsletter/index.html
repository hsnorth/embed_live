As you scroll down this page, you'll notice the header at the top becomes smaller and sticks to the top of the window. When you scroll back to the very top, it will grow back to its original, larger size. This is achieved using a combination of CSS for styling and a small amount of JavaScript to detect the scroll action.

This technique is popular on modern websites because it keeps navigation accessible without taking up too much screen space once the user starts reading the main content. The large, impactful title at the top makes a strong first impression, while the smaller, sticky version is purely functional.
