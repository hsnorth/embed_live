The rise and fall of Guzzo Cinemas

Founded in 1974

Angelo Guzzo launched the cinema group. Over time, it grew into a Quebec entertainment empire with more than 150 screens across the province.

Pandemic woes

Before COVID-19, Guzzo Cinemas had plans to expand beyond Quebec. But then the pandemic hit hard, and financial troubles mounted.

By late 2024, the Guzzo group was placed under court protection. The chain closed its remaining theatres in February 2025 after a judge declared its losses were unsustainable.

Assets on the market

The receiver Dominic Deslandes was tasked with selling off the company's assets.

Interest in the assets was strong, Deslandes said, adding that theatre chain president Vincenzo Guzzo also declared his interest. However, Guzzo has so far failed to submit a formal bid to buy them back.

First sale approved

In a July 25 ruling, the court approved the sale of a real estate asset in Laval tied to the Guzzo group. The buyer is linked to the Wilnor Group. There are two more real estate assets part of the group that are up for sale, Deslandes said.
