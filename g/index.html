What happened to Guzzo Cinemas?

Scroll right →

Lights, camera, launch (1974)

Angelo Guzzo opened his first cinema after arriving from Italy. It marked the beginning of a homegrown empire in Quebec’s movie business.

The empire builds

By the 2010s, Cinémas Guzzo boasted over 150 screens and five IMAX theatres. Vincenzo Guzzo, Angelo’s son, expressed ambitions to expand beyond Quebec to cities like Calgary and Vancouver.

Enter: the pandemic

COVID-19 hit hard, coupled with the rising trend of moviegoers turning to streaming services like Netflix.

The curtain falls

A December liquidation order marked the end of core operations. Marché Central shut in January. Seven more theatres were temporarily closed in February and assets were listed for sale.

First property sale

In June 2025, the court approved the first real estate sale — a Laval property sold to a buyer linked to the Wilnor Group. Two properties remain on the market, according to the receiver.

Vincenzo’s comeback?

Amid the asset sale, Vincenzo reopens the Terrebonne Mega-Plex and tells media he wants to revive theatres in Lacordaire and Pont-Viau. He remains optimistic.
