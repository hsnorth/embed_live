What happened to Guzzo Cinemas?

Scroll right →

Lights, camera, launch (1974)

Angelo Guzzo opened his first cinema after arriving from Italy. It marked the beginning of a homegrown empire in Quebec’s movie business.

Business is booming

By the 2010s, Cinémas Guzzo boasted over 150 screens and several IMAX theatres. Vincenzo Guzzo, Angelo’s son, expressed ambitions to expand beyond Quebec.

Enter: the pandemic

COVID-19 hit hard, coupled with the rising trend of moviegoers turning to streaming services.

The curtain falls

A late 2024 court order marked the end of core operations. Marché Central shut in January.The remaining seven theatres were temporarily closed in February and assets were listed for sale.

First property sale

In June 2025, the court approved the first real estate sale — a Laval property sold to a buyer linked to the Wilnor Group. Two real estate assets remain on the market, according to the receiver.

Vincenzo’s comeback?

Amid the asset sales, Vincenzo reopened a cinema in Terrebonne and tells media he wants to revive more.
